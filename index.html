About

Currently, I am a health communications fellow with the National Cancer Institute, following recent completion of graduate degree at the University of Michigan School of Public Health. I am leveraging my MPH, along with previous experience as a medical librarian at an academic medical research center, as well as a graphic designer to find imaginative and engaging ways to tell the stories that support public health initiatives.

I believe in the power of information to improve the health care decision making process. I believe that well organized and easily accessed information aids in understanding. I believe a beautiful design can engage the viewer, and allow for processing the information in digestible pieces. I believe a combination of understanding literacy levels and education tailored to the audience can help improve understanding, as well as contribute to changing health behaviors. I am interested in researching the areas where design, libraries and education, and public health promotion intersect.

Portfolio

Below are selected images from my portfolio of communications and design work. The work was created during my tenure at Upstate Medical University. The focus audience for the illustrated posters were patients and families of an outpatient cancer center, based on an article about common cancer myths from the National Cancer Institute. The square images were 5"x5" magnets targeted to students, and clinical staff working or studying at the hospital. The banners hang in the foyer leading to the library. The other images include a poster for hallway display, and trifold table toppers for study spaces located throughout the library.
